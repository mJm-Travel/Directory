Itinerary

Your full-day tour to the Grand Canyon West Rim from Las Vegas begins with a pickup from one of several designated hotels. Make an early start to avoid the crowds and the heat of the day. Plus a Grand Canyon Ambassador boards the bus to scan tickets so you can avoid long lines and head straight to the sights.

Unlike many tours that rush through the Grand Canyon, this one gives you plenty of time to fully appreciate the sights. Enjoy direct access to Eagle and Guano Points, and enjoy the thrill of walking on the glass floors of the Grand Canyon Skywalk (if option selected), or the aerial and canyon views from a helicopter and pontoon boat ride (if option selected). Refuel with a tasty lunch (if option selected), snacks, and bottled water will be provided to keep you hydrated in this hot climate.

There will also be time to stop at the Hoover Dam for an epic photo opportunity, and to admire the views of Lake Mead. At the end of the tour youâ€™ll be dropped off again at select Las Vegas hotels.

Great day trip from Las Vegas
Dharshani_J, Aug 2025
I enjoyed this tour a lot! It's a perfect day trip from Las Vegas and a great introduction to the Grand Canyon (West), which is the closest part of the Grand Canyon to Las Vegas. The tour guide was Jessie James and the driver was Dong and they both did a great job making sure that we had a great tour (when you tip, you should tip them separately). Dong is a careful and attentive driver. He also made sure that we got iced water bottles at each stop. Jessie has a wonderful voice (that radio announcer type of voice) and he has a gentle and pleasant way about him, which makes him a great tour guide. He was knowledgeable about all the places we were visiting and encouraged us to ask questions about the tour. The information he shared was very useful and made the tour more interesting. He also handed us an information sheet and the voucher, and explained what to expect and gave us tips on how to manage time, which was very helpful. We first stopped at the Hoover Dam, where we had 30 minutes to walk to the overlook, take photos and return to the bus. Once we got to Eagle Point, we had three hours to experience the Skywalk, explore the adjacent area and have lunch (if you purchased those options), take the shuttle to Guano point and explore that area. The restaurant can take time if there's a crowd but the food is great! The older waitress is not that pleasant though. The Skywalk isn't that big a deal though, to be honest. I don't think I got a lot out of being on that glass bridge. You get 15 minutes on it, and now they let you take your phone to take photos. If you're short for time, just skip this Skywalk. If you're also stressed about managing time, just purchase the basic entry fee package. I think I got lucky with the lines at each point, but I can see how it can get hectic trying to do everything in 3 hours. After Eagle Point, you take the shuttle to Guano Point, which has much better views than at Eagle Point. They have a place to get lunch there too, but the options are limited. Note that there can be lines at the Skywalk, the Sky View restaurant, and for the shuttle bus. So plan accordingly because 3 hours go by faster than you think. If it rains and there's lightening, they close the Skywalk. Some people in our group couldn't do the Skywalk as a result because they went to the restaurant first and by the time they were ready for the Skywalk, it was raining with lightening. So, pay attention to the weather if you book the Skywalk option. Overall, this is a tour I recommend. Thank you Jessie James and Dong!
